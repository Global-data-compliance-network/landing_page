HOW IT WORKS - THE ACTUAL FLOW

From supplier request to CB-ready submission in 3 phases:

Phase 1: request setup. You upload CSV or enter SKU, PO, supplier, and certification data. AI organizes records, maps suppliers, and lists required SC/TC fields per SKU. You set up the GOTS/GRS/OCS/RCS TC request in GDCN, add email and WhatsApp contacts, and start outreach.

Phase 2: collect and validate. AI-drafted messages go to suppliers with context and a portal link. They upload TC PDFs, scope certificates, and material data through a no-login portal. AI extracts issuer, expiry, cert ID, materials, and batches from each PDF and checks them against the standard. TC-to-PO matching then flags wrong supplier, quantity issues, expired certs, and missing fields.

Phase 3: review and outputs. One dashboard shows pass and fail, and you confirm or override AI flags until the data is clean. When checks pass, GDCN produces a pre-filled CB packet for final review and submission. The same validated evidence powers a hosted DPP with QR and exports for CSRD, EUDR, and AGEC.

Where the core IP actually lives

Expired or wrong certificates usually surface at audit, after product has already moved. GDCN automates the two hardest checks in the chain—PDF validation and TC-to-PO matching—while most competitors still push that work through manual teams.

  • AI PDF parsing + standards validation

    GDCN pulls fields from supplier TC and SC PDFs and validates them against the right standard first. Files become machine-checkable evidence before they land in your CB queue.

  • TC-to-PO matching at SKU level

    It matches extracted certificate data to PO and SKU records—supplier, material, quantities, and validity—before you submit. Teams often spend 2–3 weeks per PO doing that by hand.

  • Everything around it is infrastructure

    Outreach, reminders, the supplier portal, review UI, and exports are table stakes. Those two checks are where weeks compress to minutes and where the technical story is earned.

  • Pain solved sooner, not at audit

    Two hundred suppliers with five certs each is about 1,000 certificates in flight. GDCN flags expired or mismatched certs at upload, not when a retailer escalates or an audit fails.

  • Green Claims risk control

    The EU Green Claims Directive (2024) makes unverified sustainability claims a liability. Validation and matching before submit gives you auditable proof behind each claim.

  • No competitor fully automates both

    AI PDF parsing plus TC-to-PO matching on live supplier files is still thin in mid-market fashion. That gap will not stay open.
